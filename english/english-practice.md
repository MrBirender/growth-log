# English Communication Practice

> Goal: write clearly, articulate technical ideas precisely, communicate well in async/written contexts (PRs, Slack, docs, interviews).
> Practice is woven into every session — errors are modeled correctly, not criticized.
> Daily slot: 3:00 – 3:30 PM

---

## Two tracks

### Track 1 — Writing
One short write-up per session (100–150 words). Rotate between:
- Explain a technical concept you just learned (DSA or System Design)
- Write about your day or week in English
- Describe a problem you solved at work

### Track 2 — Speaking
Record a 2–3 minute voice note to yourself. Same prompts as writing. Listen back once. No editing — just notice what felt awkward.

---

## Weekly Review (every Sunday)
Write 150–200 words:
- What did I learn this week?
- What was confusing and why?
- What clicked that didn't before?

## Common Patterns to Watch

| Wrong | Correct | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| "not down" | "note down" | Different words — note = write, not = negation |
| "help to achieve" | "help me achieve" | "help someone do" not "help to do" |
| "i am doing dsa" | "I am doing DSA" | Capitalize "I" always; acronyms in caps |
| Missing full stops | Add periods between sentences | One idea per sentence |
| "the context of that" | "the context for that" | Preposition: "context for X" not "context of X" |
| "currecnt" | "current" | Spelling — slow down on fast typing |
| "consitent" | "consistent" | Common misspelling |
| "importnant" | "important" | Common misspelling |
| "interrest" | "interest" | Common misspelling |
| "realted" | "related" | Common misspelling |
| "buzzword" (singular) | "buzzwords" (plural) | Remember the plural |
| "problmes" | "problems" | Common misspelling |
| "desing" | "design" | Common misspelling |
| "discrmination" | "discrimination" | Common misspelling |
| "where he born" | "where he was born" | Missing auxiliary verb "was" in past tense |
| "i felt flute a bit hard" | "I found the flute a bit challenging" | "find something challenging" not "feel something hard" |
| "rest of the things were felt good" | "the rest felt good" | Remove "were" — double verb error |
| "not that much struggling was there" | "I did not struggle much with the others" | Awkward inversion — use normal subject-verb order |
| "Now comes to" | "Moving on to" | Use transition phrases for topic changes |
